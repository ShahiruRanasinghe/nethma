Horton Plains National Park

Perched amidst Sri Lanka's central highlands, Horton Plains National Park offers a contrasting landscape of montane grasslands and cloud forests. This UNESCO World Heritage site captivates visitors with its misty panoramas, cascading waterfalls, and unique flora. Home to endemic species like the sambar deer and purple-faced langur, it provides a tranquil retreat into nature's grandeur.

Sinharaja Forest Reserve

Nestled in the heart of Sri Lanka's southwestern region, Sinharaja Forest Reserve stands as a verdant sanctuary of biodiversity. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site, this tropical rainforest is a haven for endemic species, boasting a rich tapestry of flora and fauna. Its dense canopy shelters rare birds, vibrant butterflies, and elusive mammals, offering nature enthusiasts a glimpse into pristine ecosystems untouched by time. .

Minneriya National Park

Minneriya National Park unfolds a captivating tableau of wildlife spectacle, particularly renowned for its annual 'Gathering' of elephants. The expansive landscapes of this park, dotted with its reservoir, attract a myriad of bird species and support a diverse range of wildlife. Visitors can witness the harmonious interactions of creatures great and small, set against the backdrop of scenic vistas.

Bundala National Park

Bundala National Park is a mosaic of wetland habitats, teeming with avian life and diverse ecosystems. As a designated Ramsar site, it holds international importance for migratory birds, particularly flamingos that grace its waters. Beyond the birdlife, the park's diverse fauna, from crocodiles to deer, showcases the rich biodiversity sustained by its unique wetland environment.
